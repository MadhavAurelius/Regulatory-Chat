Motor Disorders (MD) involve difficulties with movement and motor coordination. These include conditions like Developmental Coordination Disorder (DCD), which impacts fine and gross motor skills, and Tic Disorders, such as Tourette Syndrome, which involve involuntary movements or sounds. Motor disorders can vary in severity and may affect daily activities, requiring therapeutic interventions to improve motor control and coordination. In India, the number of MD cases was estimated at approximately 0.027 million in CY20. By CY25, this figure had risen to 0.035 million, reflecting a CAGR of 5.96%. Further it is estimated to rise to 0.054 million cases by CY35, estimated to have grown at a CAGR of 4.48% for the CY25-35 period.
