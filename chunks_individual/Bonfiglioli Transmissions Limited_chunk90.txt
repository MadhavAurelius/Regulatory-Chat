* **23.** *    * **We may be negatively impacted by any early obsolescence of our manufacturing equipment and the** * * **spare parts or software used in such equipment.** *
We depreciate the cost of our manufacturing equipment and the spare parts or software used in such equipment over their expected useful lives. However, product cycles or manufacturing technology may change periodically, and we may decide to update our gearboxes and drive systems, parts or manufacturing processes more quickly than expected. Moreover, improvements in engineering and manufacturing expertise and efficiency may result in our ability to manufacture our gearboxes and drive systems or parts using less of our currently installed equipment and the spare parts or software used in such equipment. Alternatively, as we ramp and mature the production of our gearboxes and drive systems to higher levels, we may discontinue the use of already installed equipment and the spare parts or software used in such equipment in favour of different or additional equipment, spare part or software. The useful life of any equipment, spare parts or software that would be retired early as a result would be shortened, causing the depreciation on such equipment, spare parts or software to be accelerated, and our business, financial condition, results of operations, and cash flows may be harmed.

* **24.** *    * **Disruptions of transportation network and transportation infrastructure or deficiencies in service** * * **provided by our logistic service providers may have an adverse effect on our business, financial** * * **condition, results of operations, and cash flows.** *
We depend on transportation and logistics networks, and the connectivity and conditions of the road, rail, sea and general transportation infrastructure in India and outside in India. Delays in supply and transportation of gearboxes and drive systems to dealers, or customers due to inadequacies of the transportation infrastructure in India, disruptions in railway, road or sea transportation networks due to weather related events, labour strikes, wars or otherwise, could impact our operations. For instance, to overcome supply disruptions on account of the crisis in the Red Sea involving the Houthis in 2023, we stocked higher inventories. While we were able to continue to meet our customer orders on a timely basis due to such inventory management, there is no assurance that such delays will not occur in the future or that we will continue to be able to mitigate the impacts of such disruptions in transportation. We also rely on third parties for transportation services for both domestic sales and exports. Any failure by these service providers to transport our gearboxes and drive systems to dealers on time and in marketable condition or any service deficiency caused by these service providers could have a material impact on our operations. We may also be affected by an increase in fuel costs, as it will have a corresponding impact on freight charges levied by our third party transportation providers that may have an adverse effect on our business, financial condition, results of operations, and cash flows.
