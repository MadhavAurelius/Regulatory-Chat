## * **8.** *    * **Risks related to the Objects of the Issue** *

While all our centres operate in premises that we lease from third-parties, we incur costs towards setting up new centres which include entering into agreements with third parties such as landlords and vendors, to design and renovate our centres in accordance with our specifications and quality standards and under the time frames that we provide. We also incur costs towards personnel and operations and integrating the internal controls and technology at emerging centres, and a failure to set such sysstems up or integrate them with our wider network could result in the disruption of our ongoing operations, diversion of the attention of our management, and other unforeseen liabilities or costs. Certain risks involved in setting up new centres include the following:

a.  delays in completing renovation works in line with our internal policies and specifications; b.  breach by third parties such as vendors and landlords of their obligations under their agreements with us; c.  delay or failure to secure approvals, permits and licenses or failure to comply with the conditions of such approvals; d.  inability to obtain requisite financing at favourable costs or at all; e.  difficulties arising from coordinating and consolidating corporate and administrative functions, including the integration of internal controls and procedures; f.  recruiting and retaining clinical professionals at our centres; and g.  unforeseen legal, regulatory, contractual, labour or other issues, which could affect the profitability of our centres.
Further, there is no assurance that we will be able to identify suitable sites or targets for expansion or that we will be able to procure favourable terms or that the setting up of centres will be completed in a timely manner, or at all. In the past, we have faced certain delays in the openings of our centres, and while these delays did not have a material
