We continue to undertake projects for measured expansion of our manufacturing capacities. For instance, we propose to set up a dedicated facility in Talegaon (Maharashtra) to manufacture heavy-duty gearboxes to cater to customers under our Industry & Automation vertical.
We also seek to increasingly digitalize our operations, and our ongoing/ proposed digitalization processes include IoT implementation through a synchronized production management system, machine-to-machine communication, digitalized routine check sheets, online system with trigger and escalation levels at every station and trials on machine learning. Through this process, we expect to derive benefits such as higher efficiency through real time monitoring and controls, greater data transparency and faster response to market requirements.
In addition, we strive to source an increasing proportion of raw materials and components in our products from local/ domestic sources, including by manufacturing certain components in-house. Our efforts to increase local sourcing have historically prioritized a three-year sales forecast to determine which components to localize based on the factors such as product lead time, application of end-product, supply chain risk and total cost of ownership. We have most recently localized the production of such electric motors by manufacturing these at our facility in Mannur (Tamil Nadu). The facility commenced operations in October 2025 ensuring faster delivery of our products with a target of up to four weeks’ lead time from the date of the order, a sharp decrease from the erstwhile 12-week lead time.
We plan to similarly increase localization across other raw materials and components we consume to help lower our production cost, optimize supply chain logistics and minimize transportation lead times, as well as develop strategic partnership with our suppliers for cost control and maintenance of quality control and assurance standards.
