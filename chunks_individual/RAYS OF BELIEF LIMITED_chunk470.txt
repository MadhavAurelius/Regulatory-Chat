## **5. Support Groups and Coaching**

#### * **Programs we offer** *

We believe that families play a critical role in shaping their children’s development. Our goal is to equip parents/caregivers with the essential tools, knowledge and training to take a proactive role in their child’s therapeutic journey, fostering an environment where families can effectively support developmental progress at home. Accordingly, we also offer a parental guidance program as well as the family support program.
Our Promoter, Carving Futures Pte. Ltd., has partnered with the LEGO Foundation for the purpose of fulfilling the activities as per the project charter, which includes the Family Support Plan (“  **FSP**  ”), Play Inclusion initiatives, and the expansion of our footprint in various geographies including India, Dubai, and UAE. Pursuant to the partnership, the LEGO Foundation has contributed USD 2.02 million to our Promoter as part of a two-year project. This funding is being utilized to provide families of neurodivergent children with the tools, knowledge, and support needed— including through the use of therapeutic developmental LEGO-integrated kits and the Learning Through Play principles.
We also offer these programs online, with materials such as play kits delivered to the clients’ doorsteps, ensuring that any neurodiverse child, regardless of location, can benefit from our services.
We are focused on delivering therapies which are culturally relevant and benchmarked against global clinical standardized assessment tools. Our  training materials, guidance, and therapy modules are delivered in regional
languages, and parent training programs are tailored to Indian family structures and socio-cultural dynamics such as joint family systems, gender roles, and local caregiving customs. Our scalable model accordingly fosters a client- centric culture. Our clinical and operational oversight are centralised, ensuring that each centre adheres to our set standards of care. This allows us to optimize centre performance and improve the enrolled child and family’s experience.
