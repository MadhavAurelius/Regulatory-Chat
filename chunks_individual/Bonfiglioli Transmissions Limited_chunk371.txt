## **Quality Assurance and Quality Control**

We follow a multi-dimensional approach to quality assurance that involves ensuring quality compliance at each level of our operations, beginning with quality assurance of incoming material, followed by quality checks at the machining stage and finally at the assembly stage.
*Incoming QA.*  On receipt of materials, few samples are sent for inspection where verification of vendor documents is carried out followed by inspection of parts as per our quality plan inspection report. Identified defects are recorded and the relevant supplier is notified to change the defective material.
*Machining QA.*  Parts and materials that satisfy incoming QA are sent to the production department where the machine is set and parts are offered for “first off approval”. The team inspects the materials based on specifications and drawings and accordingly clears the item for production/ correction. In case of correction, parts are resent to QA for approval. Periodic inspections are carried out to ensure ongoing compliance.
*Assembly QA.*  To ensure compliance throughout the assembly process, the team performs various checks at periodic intervals and ensures final inspection of assembled products post the painting process. Key checkpoints include a torque audit to verify the capacities; grease calibration to verify the grease weight; oil quantity calibration to verify oil dispensation as per guidelines; fixture audit; oil quality test where samples are tested at third party laboratories to ensure compliance. This is followed by the salt spray test at a NABL laboratory to ensure compliance.
