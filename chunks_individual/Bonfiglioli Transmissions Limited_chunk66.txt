We do not typically enter into long-term agreements with suppliers and generally do not have firm commitments from our suppliers. We instead source raw materials and components on a purchase order basis and negotiate prices and quantities with our suppliers with each purchase order. As a result, we are largely dependent on our existing relationships with our suppliers. In addition, we provide detailed specifications for our raw materials and components to our suppliers, which includes drawings, documents, and samples. Any failure by our suppliers to provide parts and materials to us on time or at all, or as per our specifications and quality standards for reasons such as capacity limitations, breakdowns, or machine failures, industrial relations and safety issues, could have an adverse impact on our ability to meet our manufacturing and delivery schedules, which in turn may have an adverse impact on our operations, reputation, and ability to appropriately price our gearboxes and margins. While there have been no instances in the nine months period ended September 30, 2025 and in Fiscals 2024, 2023 and 2022, where any of our top 10 suppliers has discontinued their business with us or were unable to deliver raw materials and components to us there can be no assurance that we will not experience significant delays in the future or that our suppliers will continue to engage with us in the future. In the event our suppliers are unable to meet our delivery timelines or refuse to supply raw materials and components to us in the future we may be unable to find suitable alternatives. Furthermore, as we do not have exclusive arrangements with our suppliers, our suppliers could engage with our competitors and prioritize supplies of their other customers, which could adversely impact our ability to procure a sufficient quantity of materials at competitive rates. Any disruption in the procurement of materials could have a material adverse effect on our business, financial condition, results of operations, and cash flows.
