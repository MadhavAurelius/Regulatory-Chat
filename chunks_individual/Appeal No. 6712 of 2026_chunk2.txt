## **Appeal No. 6712 of 2026**

Alok Awasthi

:

Appellant

Vs
CPIO, SEBI, Mumbai    :  Respondent
**ORDER** 1.  The appellant had filed an application dated January 23, 2026 (received by the respondent through RTI MIS Portal) under the Right to Information Act, 2005 (“  **RTI Act**  ”). The respondent, by a letter dated January 28, 2026, responded to the application filed by the appellant. The appellant filed an appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00034) dated January 28, 2026. I have carefully considered the application, the response and the appeal and find that the matter can be decided based on the material available on record.
2.   **Queries in the application**  - The appellant, in his application dated January 23, 2026, sought the following:
*“ I, Alok Awasthi, resident of Mainpuri , hereby seek information under the RTI Act, 2005 regarding my PACL* *investments.*
*As per the SEBI PACL Refund Portal, refund applications against the following PACL Certificates have been rejected* *on the grounds that land has been allotted against these certificates as per PACL Ltd. Records*
*...........*
*Since Policy Holders have not received any land or related documents for these certificates, I request the following information:*
*1-*    *Basis of SEBIs Decision* *On what grounds did SEBI conclude that land has been allotted against PACL Certificate Numbers Mentioned above?*
*Which records or data entry from PACL Ltd. were relied upon?* *2-*    *Clarification of (YES) Declaration*
*The (YES) was declared because PACL certificates themselves mention a promise of land allotment.* *How does SEBI differentiate between a promised allotment (printed on certificate) and an actual allotment (with* *possession and documents)?* *3-*    *Details of Alleged Land Allotment* *If land is considered allotted, provide:* *Exact location (village/town, district, state).* *Survey number / plot number.* *Size and boundaries of the land.* *Provide certified copies of allotment letters, sale deeds, or possession documents issued in policy holder name.* *4-*    *Verification of Physical Possession* *Has SEBI verified whether investors actually received possession of the land?* *If not, why is refund denied solely on the basis of PACL internal records?* *5-*    *Correction of Records* *What mechanism exists for investors to challenge false (and allotted) entries in PACL database?* *How can I submit proof that no land was ever handed over?* *6-*    *Refund Eligibility* *If land allotment was only a fake promise and no physical land/document was given, why is refund not being processed?* *What steps should I take to revalidate my claim for refund?”*
