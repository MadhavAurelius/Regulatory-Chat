# Appeal Nos. 6714 & 6715 of 2026

## **ORDER**

1.  The appellant had filed two identical applications (RTI Reg. No. SEBIH/R/T/25/00234 & RTI Reg No. SEBIH/R/T/25/00233) under the Right to Information Act, 2005 (“  **RTI Act**  ”), which was transferred to the respondent from Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India and LOKPAL, on November 13 & 14, 2025, respectively. The respondent, by a common letter dated December 12, 2025 responded to the applications filed by the appellant. The appellant filed two appeals (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00036 and Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00037) dated January 29, 2026.
2.  I note that under Section 19(1) of the RTI Act, an aggrieved person may prefer the first appeal within thirty days from the receipt of the response from the CPIO of the concerned public authority. In the instant case, the impugned response from the respondent is dated December 12, 2025. The appellant, therefore, should have filed the first appeal on or before expiry of thirty days from the date of receipt of the said response. As noted above, the appellant’s first appeals were received on January 29, 2026. The first appeals have been made after the last date permissible under the RTI Act. The appellant neither made a request for condoning the said delay in filing the appeals nor made any submission explaining the reasons which caused the delay. Considering the absence of a request for condoning the delay and any valid reason that prevented the appellant from filing the appeal in time, I consider these appeal as time barred and hence, liable to be dismissed on that count.
3.  Notwithstanding the above observation, I am considering the appeals on merit. I have perused the applications and the appeals and find that the matter can be decided based on the material available on record.
