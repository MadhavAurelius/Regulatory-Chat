## **11. Psychotherapy and Counselling**

#### **Table 9: Description of Key symptoms of ADHD**
|  Category  |  Description  |
|:---|:---|
| Inattention  | -  Difficulties in maintaining focus and staying organized. - Struggle to sustain attention during tasks or  play.  -  Frequent careless mistakes in schoolwork or activities due to lack of attention to detail.  -  Hard time organizing tasks and managing time, leading to missed deadlines.  -  Avoidance or dislike of activities requiring sustained mental effort, like homework or lengthy reading  assignments.  -  Frequent misplacement of essential items (e.g., keys, books, tools).  -  Easily distracted by external stimuli (e.g., noise, movement). - Forgetfulness in daily activities (e.g.,  neglecting chores, missing appointments). |
| Hyperactivity | -  Constant movement and difficulty sitting still.  -  Fidgeting or squirming in seats; struggle to remain seated in expected situations (e.g., class, dinner  table).  -  Running or climbing in inappropriate settings, showing an inability to regulate movement.  -  Difficulty engaging in quiet or calm activities.  -  Excessive talkativeness.  -  Restlessness and feeling "on the go" or in perpetual motion.  -  Challenges adapting to structured environments.  |
| Impulsivity  | -  Hasty actions without considering consequences.  -  Difficulty waiting for turns.  -  Interrupting conversations.  |
