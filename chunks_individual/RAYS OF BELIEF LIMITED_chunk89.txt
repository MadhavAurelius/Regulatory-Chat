Failure to retain existing clients, attract new clients, or maintain relationships** * * **with referral sources may adversely impact our revenues and growth** *
Our revenues are significantly dependent on our ability to maintain relationships with existing clients, attract new clients to our programmes, and maintain relationships with referral sources including stakeholders in the communities we serve. In the six months ended September 30, 2025, and in Fiscals 2025, 2024, and 2023, we served 7,773, 8,585, 9,344 and 6,935 children, respectively. A decline in either client retention, new enrolments, or referrals from our existing network can lead to underutilization of our centres, erosion of margins, and reduced profitability. Our referral sources are not, and cannot be, obligated to refer clients to us, and may instead direct them to other service providers. Our growth and profitability therefore depend, in part, on our ability to establish and maintain close working relationships with these referral sources, comply with applicable laws and contractual arrangements with respect to such relationships, and increase awareness and acceptance of the benefits of our services.
