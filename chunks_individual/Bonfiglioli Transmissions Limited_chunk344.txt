Set forth below are case studies to showcase how we leveraged BSPA’s globally coordinated R&D functions to develop locally responsive solutions, i.e., “glocal” R&D, giving us a significant first mover advantage from such new products.
*Case Study -  Hollow Shaft Worm Gear Motor and Inline Helica*   *l*   *Gear*    *Mot*   *or*   *s*
Historically, the Indian market was dominated by conventional worm gearboxes that had solid input and output shafts that were connected using couplings, belt pulleys or chain sprockets, resulting in bulky set-up and lower system inefficiency (  *Source: F&S Report*  ).
We introduced hollow shaft worm gear motors and inline helical gear motors in the Indian market in the year 2000, leveraging BSPA’s established global manufacturing. The direct shaft-mount design enables compact, efficient installations, driving strong adoption in the Indian market. (  *Source: F&S Report*  ).
*Case Study -  Pitch Drives*
Owing to the Bonfiglioli Group’s expertise in manufacturing yaw and pitch drives globally, we were among the first companies to establish domestic manufacturing of wind-turbine pitch and yaw drives in India, beginning in 2006 at its Chennai (Tamil Nadu) facility, according to the F&S Report. With the growth of the Indian market, we have consistently expanded our drive capacities and were consequently the first to manufacture pitch drives for 1MW, 2 MW, 3MW, 4MW and 5MW turbines in India (  *Source: F&S Report*  ). This has given us a leading market share of 70% among domestic manufacturers in terms of sales values of yaw and pitch drives in the nine months period ended September 30, 2025. (  *Source: F&S Report*  ).
