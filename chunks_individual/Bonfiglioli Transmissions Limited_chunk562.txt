## * **Pricing mechanisms and OEM programme dynamics** *

Our financial performance is affected by the operating models of our key customer segments. A significant portion of our revenue in the Mobility Solutions and Wind Solutions verticals is derived from OMEs that operate on program-based procurement models. As a result, our results of operations may be influenced by changes in OEM production schedules, inventory strategies, sourcing decisions and program lifecycles, including delays, rescheduling or cancellations, which may affect our revenue, capacity utilization and margins. OEM-driven businesses may also involve longer receivable cycles and higher working capital requirements, which may affect our cash flows and return on capital employed in certain periods. While our Industry & Automation Solutions vertical benefits from a broader customer base and channel-driven sales model, our Mobility Solutions and Wind Solutions verticals may experience higher variability in performance from period to period. In addition, our profitability is influenced by pricing arrangements with customers and the timing of cost pass-through. In some cases, particularly with OEM customers, pricing revisions are governed by contractual mechanisms such as periodic reviews or price variation clauses linked to raw material indices, which may create a time lag between

changes in raw material costs and corresponding adjustments in our selling prices.
