## * **Macro-economic Overview of the Indian Economy** *

Indian economic growth in Fiscal 2025 outperformed many other major economies and was the least impacted by the inflationary pressures globally. The government has been promoting structural reforms, such as a focus on disinvestment and higher FDI limits, while also working on a national logistics policy to promote India’s economic growth post-pandemic.
India crossed the USD 3 trillion mark in Fiscal 2022 and crossed the USD 4 trillion mark in May 2025. In a realistic scenario, India needs another 2-3 years to cross the USD 5 trillion mark.
The Union Budget Fiscal 2026 outlines a total expenditure of ₹ 50.65 lakh crore with a capital outlay of ₹ 11.21 lakh crore (3.1% of GDP) and a fiscal deficit target of 4.4% of GDP.

India’s real GDP displayed resilience, with a strong rebound of 9.7% in Fiscal 2021-22 following a sharp contraction of 5.8% in Fiscal 2020-21 due to COVID-19. It maintained steady growth at 7.0% and 8.2% in Fiscal 2022-23 and Fiscal 2023-24, respectively. Projections indicate sustained growth momentum with nominal GDP projected to reach ₹540.1 trillion by Fiscal 2029- 30. Structural reforms, increasing digitalization, and expanding global trade integration underscores India’s potential to exceed USD 7.0 trillion in nominal GDP within the next decade.
