## * **8.** *    * **Risks related to the Objects of the Issue** *

While we have engaged over 339  full-time clinical professionals as on September 30, 2025, given the availability of such trained clinical professionals in the market is limited, we cannot assure you that as we expand, our network of clinical professionals will also be able to expand. We also compete with other private and public healthcare providers to attract and retain clinical professionals specifically those skilled to deal with NDDs from a limited pool of candidates. The availability in certain areas of suitably qualified personnel can be further limited in the Tier 3 cities where we operate. To the extent we cannot recruit qualified personnel, we may be required to limit the services provided by these centres, which would have a corresponding adverse effect on our results of operations, or we may be required to recruit clinical professionals from other locations, which would increase our costs as we would have to provide for their relocation.
We have also engaged certain consultants, with whom we have entered into contractual arrangements to ensure service continuity and quality standards. Any disruption in these contractual relationships, whether due to termination, non- renewal, or disputes, may result in service gaps, compromised care delivery, or reputational harm.
Increased competition for skilled clinical professionals or changes in applicable labor regulations may result in higher compensation demands or stricter contractual terms, thereby increasing operational costs. Any of these factors may adversely affect our Company’s ability to scale its operations, maintain service quality, and achieve its business objectives.
In addition to our clinical professionals we have also employed more than 80 clinical operations staff along with extensive headquarters and support staff.  *(Source: CARE Report, on page 63)*  . The loss of the services of such professionals and staff or our inability to recruit or train a sufficient number of experienced clinical professionals or staff or our inability to manage the attrition levels in different employee categories may have an adverse effect on our financial results and business prospects.
As of September 30, 2025, the Company had a total 517 permanent employees and 15 consultants, of which 339 are full-time clinical professionals (including psychologists, therapists, and special educators). For further details, please see  *“Our Business - Our Team”*  on page 242  *.*
