## **7. Shareholding pattern of our Company**

#### * **Details of the utilisation of Net Proceeds** *

We intend to continue building our presence through centres with a emphasis in Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities to target the large, underserved communities in these regions which lack access to adequate developmental care facilities for NDDs. Since all our centres are opened on lease basis, our business model allows us to roll out new centres with optimal upfront investment, limited capital expenditure and high scalability. Our RoCE for Fiscals 2023, 2024 and 2025 was (186.10)%, 4.58% and 7.49%, respectively. As we scale our business, we intend to continue focusing on the lower risk, asset-light model for procuring centre space and managing our administrative expenses such as rent for our leased centres, to improve our operational and cost efficiency and profitability.
We intend to utilize ₹576.20 million of the proceeds raised from the Issue for setting up 414  new centres between Fiscal 2027 and Fiscal 2029. As part of our growth strategy, we also intend to expand our domestic as well as international presence through inorganic growth opportunities such as acquisitions and strategic alliances such as our “  *Company Learning Centres in partnership with Licensed Professionals*  ” model. For further details, see  *“Our* *Business -  Our Growth Strategies”*  on page 230.
The rationale for the increase in the number of centres is aligned with the Company’s growth strategy and the demand in the behavioural health and neurodevelopmental care segment. The Company’s network of centres has expanded from 71 centres in Fiscal 2023 to 136 centres across 57 cities in 20 states and union territories as of September 30, 2025, reflecting the Company’s ability to successfully scale operations. This expansion has been driven by growing awareness of Neurodevelopmental Disorders, such as Autism Spectrum Disorder, ADHD, Down Syndrome, and Learning Disabilities, coupled with limited availability of structured, affordable intervention services in India. The Company’s experience shows strong client traction, the Company served 6,935 children in Fiscal 2023 and 8,585 children in Fiscal 2025, which underscores the need for additional capacity. (  *Source: CARE Report, on page 62*  )
Further, our Company operates on an asset-light model. Of the total expenditure of ₹1.64 million allocated for a Tier I city center, ₹1.37 million for a Tier II city center, and ₹1.22 million for a Tier III city center, a substantial portion of these costs is attributed to fit-out costs, which essential for creating an appropriate environment for child-focused therapy services. We are primarily focused on expanding in Tier II and Tier III cities, where the costs are comparatively lower. For School Collaboration Centres, the investment is even more modest, with costs typically around ₹ 0.46 million. In particular, Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities remain underserved, with inadequate access to neurodevelopmental care facilities. By increasing the number of centres, the Company aim to address this gap and meet the rising demand for early intervention and family-centric care.
