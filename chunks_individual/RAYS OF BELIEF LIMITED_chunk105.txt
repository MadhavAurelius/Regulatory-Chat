## * **8.** *    * **Risks related to the Objects of the Issue** *

* **20.** *    * **Low public awareness, limited target age group, and social stigma associated with NDDs may restrict demand for** * * **our services and limit our growth.** *
The Company was founded, primarily to address key barriers related to NDDs in the behavioural health domain, which continues to face challenges arising from low public awareness and the widespread social stigma associated with developmental disabilities. In many regions, particularly semi-urban and rural areas, misconceptions, cultural sensitivities, and lack of reliable information may discourage a potential client from seeking early diagnosis or intervention services for children with NDDs.
Even in cases where a diagnosis is made, families may choose not to avail of our services due to prevailing stigma, thereby reducing our potential client base. Since our services consist of early intervention programs for children up to age 15, this limits our addressable market to a specific age group, and the impact of stigma and low awareness can further reduce uptake. These factors may lead to underutilization of our existing centres, slow the pace of geographical expansion. Such constraints could adversely affect our revenue, growth prospects, and long-term operational sustainability.

* **21.** *    * **Low enrolment rates, program discontinuation, and payment constraints may adversely impact our operational** * * **efficiency, revenues, and profitability** *
We prepare intervention plans for children with NDDs which are personalised based on each child’s unique needs and condition severity. The individual performance of each child depends on various factors, including personal ability, family environment and physical health, all of which impacts the success of the plans prepared by us.
Our Company aims to make our programs accessible to all regardless of their means and accordingly allows for variations in our payment terms as per the needs of the family. Health insurance plans do not cover our services, making clients solely responsible for the entire cost of our programs. The growth of client receivables, or deterioration in the ability to collect on these accounts due to the significant financial burden placed on clients, could have an adverse effect on our business, results of operations, and financial condition. If our clients experience dissatisfaction with services, or dispute the pricing of our programmes, we may find it difficult to increase the use of our services within our existing client base and it may be more difficult to attract new clients. While we have a strict refund policy which restricts refunding to only certain services, we may still be required to grant refunds, any of which could negatively impact our operating results and harm our business.
