## **INTERNAL RISKS**

If we fail to accurately forecast order volumes or plan production schedules, we may experience capacity utilisation inefficiencies that could impact our operational performance and financial results. Since a portion of our gearboxes and drive systems are manufactured for direct customers exclusively on order, our production planning relies on demand forecasting rather than inventory management to that extent. Timing mismatches between production planning cycles and fluctuating order patterns may create operational disruptions affecting manufacturing schedules, increasing costs, delaying deliveries, and potentially damaging our market reputation, thereby adversely affecting our business, financial condition, results of operations, and cash flows.

* **11.** *    * **Our ongoing and proposed capacity addition plans are subject to the risk of unanticipated delays in** * * **implementation and cost overruns.** *
We have made and intend to continue making investments to add capacities through additional manufacturing facilities close to our existing facility in Maharashtra to aid our growth efforts. We have recently commenced manufacturing electric motors at our facility in Mannur (Tamil Nadu) in October 2025 and intend to establish a new manufacturing facility in Talegaon (Maharashtra), adjacent to our existing operational facility to focus on manufacturing heavy-duty gearboxes. The land in Talegaon (Maharashtra) on which we propose to establish our new facility is currently held by one of our Group Companies, Bonfiglioli Drive Solutions Private Limited (“  **BDSPL**  ”), under lease agreement entered into by BDSPL with MIDC. We are in the process of acquiring the leasehold rights from BDSPL which remains subject to approvals from MIDC and there can be no assurance that the leasehold will be transferred from BDSPL to us within the anticipated timelines or at all.
Our expansion plans remain subject to the potential problems and uncertainties that construction projects face including cost overruns or delays. Problems that could adversely affect our expansion plans include labour shortages, increased costs of equipment or manpower, delays or defects in design or construction, the possibility of unanticipated future regulatory restrictions, incremental preoperative expenses, taxes and duties, interest and finance charges, working capital margin, environment and ecology costs and other external factors which may not be within the control of our management. We may also face interruptions in the supply of electricity and water required for our construction efforts and operations. Further, there might be price fluctuations and the currently estimated project costs may increase on account of factors beyond our control, including increase in cost of machinery and associated transportation or other charges or taxes. Failure to complete the development on time and within budget could adversely affect our business, financial condition, results of operations, and cash flows.
