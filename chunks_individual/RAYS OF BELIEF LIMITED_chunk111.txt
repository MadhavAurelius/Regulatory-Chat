* **28.** *    * **Our business and ability to provide our services is highly dependent upon the recruitment, training and retention** * * **of employees. Thus, employee benefit expense constitute a major portion of our expenses contributing up to 58.34%** * * **and 49.69% from our total expenses in Fiscal 2025 and the six months period ended September 30, 2025. Such a** * * **significant increase in this cost could lead to lower profitability.** *
Our Company’s operations are heavily reliant on the availability, retention, and performance of clinical professionals. The specialized nature of this sector necessitates a high standard of care, which can only be achieved through continuous investment in recruiting, training, and retaining qualified staff. As a result, employee benefit expenses represent a significant portion of our Company’s overall cost structure.
The employee cost for the six months period ended September 30, 2025 and for the last three fiscals ending for March 2025, 2024, and 2023 are as follows:
|  Particulars  |  Six months  period ended  September 30,  2025  |  Fiscal 2025  |  Fiscal 2024  |  Fiscal 2023  |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| Employee cost (in ₹ million)  | 205.07  | 211.11  | 159.67  | 102.25  |
| Employee cost as a percentage of total expenses (%) | 49.69%  | 58.34%  | 52.08%  | 33.28%  |

Any substantial increase in these expenses, whether due to market competition for qualified clinical professionals, statutory changes, inflationary pressures, or the need to expand personnel in line with business growth could materially impact our profitability. If our general employee and related expenses increase, we may not be able to raise the fee charged for our therapy plans correspondingly.
