## **INTERNAL RISKS**

Our gearboxes and drive systems may have defects that may require us to remedy these defects through recalls, replacements, repairs or repurchases. In addition, we could be subject to civil or criminal penalties or may incur costs for recall, replacement, repairs or repurchases. Concerns about the quality and safety of our gearboxes and drive systems, whether raised internally or by regulators, could result in production delays, recalls, loss of sales,

governmental investigations, regulatory action, claims, litigation and settlements and reputational damage. This in turn could result in substantial damage to brand and consumer trust in our gearboxes and drive systems.
While we have not experienced any product recalls in the nine months period ended September 30, 2025, and in Fiscals 2024, 2023, 2022, any perceived or actual defect in our gearboxes, drive systems or after-sale services provided by our dealers or third parties could also result in customer claims for damages. In defending such a claim, we could incur substantial costs and be subject to negative publicity. In addition, management resources could be diverted from our business towards defending such claims. As a result, our business, results of operations, financial condition and cash flows could suffer. Furthermore, our insurance does not cover product liability costs and losses related to the recall of gearboxes and drive systems for design defects or replacement of parts. While our purchase conditions under the purchase orders with critical suppliers include indemnification provisions for liabilities arising from product defects, such recourses are subject to limitations set for the contracts, and there is no assurance that we will be able to claim losses from suppliers due to product liability claims or recalls resulting for defective supplies.
