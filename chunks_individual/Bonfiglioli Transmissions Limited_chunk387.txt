## * **Export Promotion Capital Goods Scheme, 2020** *

The Central Electricity Authority (Measures relating to Safety and Electric Supply) Regulations, 2023 (“  **Electricity Regulations**  ”) were issued by the Central Electricity Authority (“  **CEA**  ”) under the Electricity Act, 2003, and provide for,  *inter alia*  , comprehensive safety requirements for the construction, installation, operation, protection, maintenance, inspection and testing of electrical plants, lines and installations. The Electricity Regulations apply to all electrical installations and to all persons engaged in the generation, transmission, distribution, trading, supply, or use of electricity. Under the Electricity Regulations, stakeholders must ensure competent governance of electrical safety, comply with structured inspection and testing regimes and obtain prescribed approvals prior to energization in specified cases, such as for the installation of diesel generators. They also require incident reporting for major failures of electrical installations or electrical accidents, and robust record-keeping. Non-compliance with the Electricity Regulations can result in refusal of energization/approval, directions to rectify, suspension of operations where applicable, disconnection of installations and penalties under the Electricity Act.
Additionally, in the states where we operate and maintain manufacturing facilities, our electricity usage is governed by state-specific statutes, including the Maharashtra Electricity Duty Act, 2016, and the Tamil Nadu Tax on Consumption or Sale of Electricity Act, 2003.
