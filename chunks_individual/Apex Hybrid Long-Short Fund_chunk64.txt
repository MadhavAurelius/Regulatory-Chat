## **1. Risk mitigation strategies for investments with each kind of originator**

**Bankruptcy Risk:**  If the originator of securitized debt instruments in which the Investment Strategy invests is subject to bankruptcy proceedings and the court in such proceedings concludes that the sale of the assets from originator to the trust was not a 'true sale', and then the Investment Strategy could experience losses or delays in the payments due.
Risk Mitigation: Normally, specific care is taken in structuring the securitization transaction so as to minimize the risk of the sale to the trust not being construed as a 'true sale'. It is also in the interest of the originator to demonstrate the transaction as a true sell to get the necessary revenue recognition and tax benefits.
**Limited Liquidity and Price risk** Presently, secondary market for securitized papers is not very liquid. There is no assurance that a deep secondary
