## **5. Support Groups and Coaching**

#### **Our Centres**

Firstly, they provide mentorship and guidance to our clinical team, thereby enhancing the quality and efficacy of therapeutic interventions.
Secondly, these professionals act as crucial navigators for parents, offering comprehensive consultations, addressing parental concerns, and facilitating an understanding of care pathways for their children. This approach ensures both quality assurance for our therapists and fosters trust and confidence among parents.
The operational structure and revenue model for these type of centres are as follows:
i.  Operational Structure:
At our  *“Company Learning Centres in partnership with Licensed Professionals”*  , our Company and the Licensed Professionals jointly participate in the clinical and operational delivery, forming a consolidated care team. While the Licensed Professionals bring local presence and provide referrals to our services, our Company provides our therapy services, along with standardized protocols, training, and a brand-aligned set up.
We establish  *“EICs”*  in collaboration with Licensed Professionals. As part of our  *“EIC”*  model, we send our qualified clinical professionals to existing establishments of Licensed Professionals only two to three times a week to undertake assessments for early identification of NDD symptoms, developmental screening, parent awareness, and short-term intervention planning. In the event  we determine that a child needs further diagnosis or therapy, such children are referred to visit our  *“Company Learning Centres” or “Company Learning Centres* *in partnership with Licensed Professionals”*  , as the case may be.
ii.  Revenue Model:
Under this model, our Company and the Licensed Professional share the financial proceeds in line with mutually agreed commercial terms, which may be structured as profit-sharing or revenue percentage-based models.
At our  *EICs*  , the revenue is primarily generated through developmental assessments, consultations, and awareness programs. In some cases, these centres act as referral hubs to our larger learning centres, strengthening the intake funnel and community outreach. Revenue is shared between our Company and the Licensed Professional based on mutually agreed terms, which may include profit-sharing, or percentage-based models. The Licensed Professional is also entitled to a professional honorarium for their continued engagement and
support in spreading awareness, facilitating connections, and promoting the services offered by our Company. The honorarium amount shall be determined based on the number of successful patient engagements facilitated through the physician's initiative and may be reviewed periodically.
