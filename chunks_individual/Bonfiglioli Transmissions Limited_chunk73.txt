We typically engage with our customers through a combination of long-term framework agreements, annual contracts and purchase order-based arrangements. While we have framework agreements with certain key customers that establish commercial terms and pricing mechanisms, these agreements do not always guarantee minimum purchase volumes or commitments. A significant portion of our revenue is generated through purchase orders placed by customers based on their requirements, project timelines and market conditions. As a result, we are dependent on our existing customer relationships and our ability to maintain our position as a preferred supplier to our customers.
Any reduction in orders from, or loss of, any of our major customers due to factors such as changes in their business strategies, financial difficulties, supply chain diversification, technological changes, competitive pressures, or their decision to manufacture components in-house or source from alternative suppliers, could have a material adverse effect on our revenue and profitability. Furthermore, customer concentration exposes us to the risk that deterioration in the financial condition or business prospects of any of our major customers could result in delayed payments, payment defaults, or cancellation of orders, which could adversely impact our working capital, cash flows and financial performance.
