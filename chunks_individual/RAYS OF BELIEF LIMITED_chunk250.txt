## **2. Expenditure for lease payments for our existing centres in India:**

As of September 30, 2025, the Company operated 136 centres in India. These centres are set up through:
-  Leased premises, acquired on a leasehold basis

-  Spaces provided by Licensed Professionals partnered with the Company

-  Collaborations with schools, under formal arrangements
These are governed by lease or leave-and-license agreements, typically ranging from 11 months to three years. For further details, see  *“Our Business -  Properties”*  on page 250.
The rationale for this object: (i) Securing continuity of operations: Our Centres are the primary delivery channel for services. Timely discharge of lease obligations ensures uninterrupted access to premises and avoids disruption of operations due to potential, which could materially impact our ability to serve families; (ii) Managing predictable obligations: Lease rentals constitute a recurring contractual liability. Allocating proceeds towards these obligations provides greater financial flexibility for the Company to focus on growth and expansion while safeguarding operational stability. Accordingly, allocating proceeds for lease payments supports business continuity, aligns with comparable industry practice, and enhances transparency in utilization of funds.
