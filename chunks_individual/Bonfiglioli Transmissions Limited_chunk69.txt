## **INTERNAL RISKS**

-  forced or voluntary closure, including as a result of regulatory inspections or natural or man-made

 disasters at our facilities;
-  manufacturing shutdowns or equipment breakdown or failure and industrial accidents;
-  labour disputes, strikes or lock-outs;
-  local community, political or other opposition;
-  shortage of personnel;
-  interruptions in supply of water and electricity or other resources;
-  changes in applicable laws and regulations impacting our operations;
-  failure or bottlenecks in production processes, especially due to inadequate supply of utilities such as

 power and water.
There is no assurance that our business and financial results may not be adversely affected by any disruption of operations at our manufacturing facilities, including as a result of any of the factors mentioned above.
While we have not experienced significant disruptions in our manufacturing operations in the nine months period ended September 30, 2025 and in Fiscals 2024, 2023 and 2022, there can be no assurance that we will not face disruptions in the future.
Any prolonged disruptions at our manufacturing facilities could result in a lower capacity utilisation, which in turn could have an adverse impact on our operations, our ability to meet our production schedules and our business, financial condition, results of operations, and cash flows.
