## * **India's Competitiveness among the Leading Manufacturing Economies** *
India’s Competitive Edge:
*Global Ranking and Growth Potential*  - India is the fifth-largest manufacturing economy, rapidly advancing under the Make in India initiative.
o   **Cost Competitiveness:**  Significantly lower labor costs than China and Mexico.
o   **Demographic Advantage:**  India is expected to add over 140 million new middle- and high-income earners by Fiscal 2030, expanding the consuming class to nearly 800 million people.
o   **Skilled Workforce and Technology Adoption -**  India’s large skilled workforce and rising adoption of Industry 4.0 technologies - IoT, AI, and robotics are enhancing productivity.
o   **Global Value Chain Integration -**  Trade partnerships and infrastructure initiatives like Gati Shakti and the Dedicated Freight Corridors are strengthening logistics capabilities.
o   **Monetary Policy Easing -**  Monetary policy has eased, with the RBI cutting repo rates by 25 bps and adopting an accommodative stance, with further cuts expected.
o   **Strategic Trade Agreements / Foreign Trade Policy -**  India is advancing trade deals such as the India-UAE CEPA and the Australia ECTA to boost exports amid rising tariff barriers, while ongoing UK-EU negotiations and updated Foreign Trade Policy enhance global market access.
