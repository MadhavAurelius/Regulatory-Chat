## * **8.** *    * **Risks related to the Objects of the Issue** *

* **22.** *    * **Our “Upskilling Academy” initiative currently does not generate direct revenue from operations and may continue** * * **to operate as a cost center, which could adversely affect our margins and results of operations.** *
We have established Upskilling Academies to provide specialized training and professional development for our clinical and operational staff. For the six months period ended September 30, 2025 and for the Fiscals 2025, 2024, and 2023, these Upskilling Academies have not contributed to our Revenue from Operations. While we believe these academies are essential for maintaining service quality and clinical standards, there can be no assurance that they will generate direct revenue in the future or that the costs associated with maintaining them will be offset by increased operational efficiencies. Any inability to effectively manage the costs of these academies or to realize the intended clinical benefits could impact our financial condition.
* **23.** *    * **None of the directors of our Company possess formal qualifications or specialized expertise directly related to the** * * **specific business activities and industry in which our Company operates.** *
While the members of our Board collectively bring extensive experience in areas such as finance, management, healthcare, law, commerce, administration, and corporate governance, no one have obtained formal training or qualifications (clinically) held positions that are directly aligned with the technical or operational aspects of our business sector. Given the nature of our industry, effective leadership often requires a deep understanding of market dynamics, regulatory frameworks, technological developments, and competitive forces specific to this sector. The absence of directors with such specialized industry background may limit the ability of our Board to anticipate and respond effectively to sector-specific challenges or emerging opportunities. This could, in turn, impact on the strategic decision-making process and operational oversight of our Company.
While our management team is supported by experienced professionals who handle the day-to-day affairs of the business, reliance on external advisors or functional experts for specialized matters may increase operational costs and elongate the decision-making process. Consequently, any inability of the Board to offer industry-specific guidance and insight could adversely affect our Company’s business operations.
