# Appeal No. 6704 of 2026

## **ORDER**

1.  The appellant had filed an application dated January 01, 2026 (received by the respondent through RTI MIS Portal) under the Right to Information Act, 2005 (“  **RTI Act**  ”). The respondent, by a letter dated January 19, 2026, responded to the application filed by the appellant. The appellant filed an appeal (Reg. No. SEBIH/A/E/26/00026) dated January 21, 2026. I have carefully considered the application, the response and the appeal and find that the matter can be decided based on the material available on record.
2.   **Queries in the application**  - The appellant, in his application dated January 01, 2026, sought the following information regarding allotment process in the Initial Public Offering (IPO) of E2E Networks Limited, listed on NSE Emerge SME Platform:
*“ A. GENERAL IPO DETAILS:*
*Complete details of the IPO issue including:*
*Issue opening and closing dates*
*Issue size and price band*
*Total number of shares offered under QIB category*
*Total applications received under QIB category*
*Total number of shares applied for under QIB category*
*Final subscription status category-wise under QIB category*
*B. QIB CATEGORY ALLOTMENT DETAILS:*
*Complete basis of allotment document/report prepared by the Registrar (MUFG InTime India Private Limited) specifically* *for the QIB category, including:*
*Methodology adopted for allotment in QIB category*
*Whether proportionate allotment basis was followed as per SEBI (ICDR) Regulations*
*Detailed working of the allotment formula used*
*Category-wise bifurcation of QIB applicants (Mutual Funds, Insurance Companies, FPIs, etc.)*
*Total number of QIB applications received and their details:*
*Number of applications received*
*Number of valid applications*
*Number of applications rejected (with reasons)*
*Minimum and maximum application size in QIB category*
*Final allotment statistics for QIB category:*
*Total number of QIBs allotted shares*

*Details of any discretionary allotment, if applicable*
*C. PROCESS AND COMPLIANCE VERIFICATION:*
*Copy of the final basis of allotment approval granted by the NSE Emerge/Stock Exchange for the QIB category.*
*Confirmation whether the allotment in QIB category was done on proportionate basis as mandated under Regulation 253 of* *SEBI (ICDR) Regulations, 2018, or any other applicable regulation for SME platform.*

3.   **Reply of the Respondent**  - The respondent, in response to the queries in the application, informed that the information sought is not maintained by SEBI in normal course of regulation of securities market. Hence, the same is not available with SEBI.
4.   **Ground of appeal**  -  The appellant, in his appeal, has disputed the reply of the respondent that the requested information is not available with SEBI. Further, the appellant has contended that his application ought to have been transferred to concerned public authority under Section 6(3) of RTI Act, if certain information was not available with SEBI.
5.  I have perused the application and the response provided thereto. The respondent, in his response, has mentioned that the requested information is not maintained by SEBI in normal course of regulation of securities market and hence, not available with SEBI. On consideration, with regard to queries under the headings,  *A. GENERAL IPO DETAILS; B. QIB CATEGORY ALLOTMENT DETAILS and C.* *PROCESS AND COMPLIANCE VERIFICATION,*  I concur with the response of respondent that the information sought is not maintained by SEBI under the normal course of regulation of securities market and hence, not available with the respondent. I note that the respondent can only provide the information that is available with him. In this context, I note that the Hon’ble Supreme Court of India in  *Central Board* *of Secondary Education & Anr. vs. Aditya Bandopadhyay & Ors*  (Judgment dated August 9, 2011) held that  *“The* *RTI Act provides access to all information that is available and existing. This is clear from a combined reading of section 3* *and the definitions of `information' and `right to information' under clauses (f) and (j) of section 2 of the Act. If a public* *authority has any information in the form of data or analysed data, or abstracts, or statistics, an applicant may access such* *information, subject to the exemptions in section 8 of the Act. But where the information sought is not a part of the record of* *a public authority, and where such information is not required to be maintained under any law or the rules or regulations of* *the public authority, the Act does not cast an obligation upon the public authority, to collect or collate such non-available* *information and then furnish it to an applicant*  .” Further, I note that the Hon’ble Central Information Commission (hereinafter referred to as “  **CIC**  ”), in the matter of  *Lakshminarayanan R vs. SEBI*  (order dated

6.  Further, with regard to queries under the heading “  *D. REGULATORY OVERSIGHT”,*  I find that the same has not been adequately addressed. Accordingly, I am of the view that the aforementioned queries warrant reconsideration.
7.  The appellant, in his appeal, has also requested that his application be transferred to the concerned public authority. I note that as per the current position, NSE and the listed company E2E Networks Ltd are all private entities and are not public authority within the meaning of section 2(h) of RTI Act. Hence, the aforementioned entities would not fall within the purview of the RTI Act. In the context of applicability of RTI Act to NSE, I note that the Hon’ble CIC, in the matter of  *R  S  Malik  vs.  CPIO, SEBI  and  Another* (Order dated April 27, 2023) , observed that  *“The Commission  at  the  outset  clarified  during  the  hearing  that* *the  matter  regarding  applicability  of  the  RTI  Act,  2005  to National  Stock  Exchange  of  India  Limited  (NSE)* *is  sub  judice  and  has  not  attained  finality.  The  applicability  of  the RTI Act, 2005 to NSE was affirmed by the* *Single Bench of the Hon’ble High Court of Delhi in National Stock Exchange v. Central Information Commission, W.P(C)* *4748/2007 vide order dated 15.04.2010. However, the division bench  of  the  Hon'ble  High  Court  of  Delhi,  in* *National  Stock  Exchange  v.  Central  Information  Commission,  LPA 315/2010  vide  order  dated  04.05.2010,* *stayed  the  aforementioned  judgment  which  had  interpreted  NSE  as  a  "public authority" under the RTI Act, 2005.* *Therefore, the Commission held that as per the current legal position, the NSE does not  come  under  the  purview  of  the* *RTI  Act,  2005......”*  Accordingly, I find that intervention of this forum is not necessary in this regard.
